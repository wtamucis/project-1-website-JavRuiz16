Five Quick Wins

Small adjustments pay off fast on dirt. These tips focus on control, efficiency, and enjoyment—because a fun run today makes it easier to run again tomorrow. Consider this your low‑friction checklist before heading out.

Remember that pace on trails is slower than roads. Judge effort with breathing and conversation, not your road splits. It’s normal to walk steep grades and shuffle tricky descents.

Keep notes after each run about terrain, shoes, and fueling. Patterns emerge quickly and help you dial things in.

  1. Start easy: choose short, loopable routes with good footing. Build time‑on‑feet before speed.
  2. Footwork first: shorten your stride, quicken cadence. Think light, quiet steps.
  3. Hydrate smart: bring water for anything >45–60 minutes; add electrolytes in heat or humidity.
  4. Etiquette: yield to uphill traffic, announce passes, and leash dogs where required.
  5. Safety: check weather, tell someone your route, carry a small kit (band‑aid, tape, gel, whistle).
